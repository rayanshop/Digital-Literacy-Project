================================================================
  TASK 4 – PART B: SOCIAL MEDIA DO'S AND DON'TS CHECKLIST
  Module 4 | Digital Communication Etiquette
  For: College Students | Role: Digital Ambassador
================================================================

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
✅  DO'S – Responsible Social Media Practices
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

[ ] 1. DO think before you post.
        Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor,
        parent, or future employer saw this?" If unsure, don't
        post it.

[ ] 2. DO keep your privacy settings updated.
        Review who can see your posts, your profile, and your
        tagged photos regularly. Limit personal information
        visible to strangers.

[ ] 3. DO give credit when sharing others' content.
        Tag original creators, mention sources, and respect
        copyright when reposting images, articles, or videos.

[ ] 4. DO use a professional tone in public comments and
        discussions.
        Debate ideas respectfully. You can disagree without
        being rude or dismissive.

[ ] 5. DO verify information before sharing it.
        Cross-check news stories or claims through reliable
        sources before you repost. Misinformation spreads fast.

[ ] 6. DO maintain separate personal and professional profiles
        where possible.
        Use LinkedIn for career networking and keep your
        public-facing profiles portfolio-appropriate.

[ ] 7. DO report harmful or abusive content.
        If you see bullying, hate speech, or dangerous content,
        use the platform's report tools rather than engaging
        with it directly.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
❌  DON'TS – Behaviours to Avoid Online
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

[ ] 1. DON'T share personal details publicly.
        Avoid posting your phone number, home address, daily
        schedule, or financial information on public profiles.

[ ] 2. DON'T engage in cyberbullying or online harassment.
        Never send threatening messages, pile on with negative
        comments, or participate in "cancel" campaigns targeting
        individuals.

[ ] 3. DON'T post under the influence of strong emotions.
        Avoid posting when you are angry, upset, or impulsive.
        Messages sent in the heat of the moment can cause
        lasting damage to relationships and reputation.

[ ] 4. DON'T spread unverified rumours or fake news.
        Sharing inaccurate information — even accidentally —
        can harm individuals and communities. Always check
        your sources first.

[ ] 5. DON'T overshare private information about others.
        Posting photos, locations, or personal details of
        friends/classmates without their consent is a
        violation of their privacy.

[ ] 6. DON'T use social media as a substitute for direct,
        important conversations.
        Serious matters (conflicts, apologies, sensitive news)
        should be handled in person or via a direct call —
        not a public post or group chat.

[ ] 7. DON'T ignore copyright and intellectual property.
        Do not use images, music, or written content without
        permission or proper attribution.
